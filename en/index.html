✦ About Me
I am from Fuyang, Anhui Province. In 2020, I graduated from high school with a ranking of 6,500 in Anhui Province's science track and came to Shanghai to study Computer Science at East China University of Science and Technology. After four years, I continued my graduate studies, focusing on Computer Vision research.
Since childhood, I have been passionate about poetry, music, history, and literature. During high school, I immersed myself in Tang poetry, the Book of Songs, classical Chinese prose anthologies, and the Records of the Grand Historian. I particularly enjoyed classical Chinese writing and often composed essays for exams, though my writing was initially chaotic and nearly disastrous. With persistent effort, I gradually improved my skills.
Since university, I have frequently written poems and short verses. In pursuit of quality, I often polish them late into the night, finding great joy in this process. However, my poetry has two regrets: First, whenever inspiration strikes, I either jot it down hastily like Li He storing ideas, or casually share them on social media. Therefore, most of my rough drafts are scattered and countless. Only one or two out of ten can be considered "complete poems." Though I have tried to organize them, trivial matters keep intervening—this is my first regret. Second, poetry should primarily convey emotions and meaning, with meter and rhyme being refinements. Unfortunately, I lack mastery of prosody, so my verse inevitably has technical flaws and cannot reach elegant heights—this is my second regret.
Despite these regrets, my enthusiasm remains undiminished. Through constant accumulation, careful excerpting, meticulous refinement of phrases, and deliberate word choice, I have gained much. My poetry improves daily, far surpassing my earlier works. When I read my old pieces today, I still feel embarrassed. I imagine that when I look back at today's works in the future, this feeling will be even stronger, yet I will cherish these memories.
By nature, I am quiet and reserved, modest and prudent. Therefore, I have few acquaintances, but most are worthy people.

✿ Poetry
Response to a Netizen's Image of Two Suns over Tianshui
Hard to tell which crimson orb is sun or water,
Unknown if rising dawn or setting dusk approaches.
Arms raised high still shouting "Long live!"
Capitalist or socialist, each takes a direction.
Rivers and mountains change through hundred generations,
The republic's glory spans the long years.
If different paths lead homeward still,
What harm in several transformations?
Winter Reflections
Plane trees whisper low,
Agreeing to meet the dusk.
This cold air is severe indeed,
Yet we can still await the coming spring.
Partridge Sky
How pitiful that time passes too hastily,
Just realized autumn aged, winter arrives again.
Lonely lamp shadow falls beneath the plane tree,
Broken leaves drift wandering in the north wind.
Two thousand miles, eight hours' journey,
Kindred spirits in one place, two hearts connected.
Graceful snow falls on this southern traveler,
At happiness's door we embrace once more.
In the Night
I too, as usual, intoxicated daily,
Night after night reaching joy until dawn.
Fame and profit need not bind me,
Earth's sounds and heaven's sounds escort my youth.
Dispatching a Messenger Through the Air
Horses tire, mountains grow more distant,
Rain hastens, shoulders bear heavier weight.
Many weary travelers at the world's edge,
Oxen and horses bearing lychees for men.
Autumn
Moon nears mid-heaven, spirits rise higher,
Wild songs and drunken dance with golden cups.
Small sips of clear brew, I'm getting drunk,
Half autumn moon, half autumn wind.
Untitled
Poetry materials flow from my mouth as praise,
I'm no genius, I carve and polish myself.
Summer, autumn, winter, spring—flowers, snow, moon,
Cultivating elegant taste to share with you.
Vernacular Farewell
Bustling passersby at the station,
Endless sunset beyond the street.
Looking back on the past thirty days,
How much sorrow fills my heart.
Today departing in such haste,
Spring and autumn, how long they are.
Moving like distant stars, hard to gather,
Helpless—what can be done?
After Scores Released
Forest birds startle morning dreams,
Worried heart deep as the sea.
Waking from dreams, knowing helplessness,
Alone facing the fresh dawn sky.
